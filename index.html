The 4 Master Patterns behind every GST exemption
Every exam question can be cracked with these four lenses.
Pattern 1 — "Genuine need, no luxury upgrade"
Basic version of a service = exempt. Upgraded/luxury version = taxable. AC coach vs non-AC. Room ₹999/day vs ₹1,000/day. Economy class vs business class. ₹7,500 maintenance vs ₹8,000.
Pattern 2 — "Primary purpose must be noble"
If the dominant activity is exempt (education, health, charity), the whole bundle is exempt. But if a trust is renting shops or putting ads — that's commercial activity bolted on to charity — taxable.
Pattern 3 — "State institutions, public benefit"
Government providing services in its sovereign capacity = exempt. But when government acts like a business (renting property, speed post, police for private events) = taxable. The test: is this something only a state can do?
Pattern 4 — "Preserve the supply chain, not the final product"
Agricultural exemptions protect the farm-to-market chain. The moment a service transforms produce into a value-added product (rice→rice flour, potato→chips, tomato→ketchup), it's no longer just transport/storage — it's manufacturing. Taxable.
Exam shortcut: When you see a borderline case, ask — "Is this serving the poor/sick/student/farmer in their basic capacity? Or is there a commercial motive hiding here?" That answer is almost always the GST answer.
Charitable Activities — Sec 12AA / 12AB registered entities
Memory hook: PAEP — Public Health · Advancement of Religion/Yoga · Educational Programmes for marginalized · Preservation of Environment. Only these 4 qualify as "charitable activities."
Exempt
Yoga / spirituality / meditation camps
HIV counseling, addiction care, disability support
Education: orphans, prisoners, abused persons, rural 65+
Forest/wildlife preservation
Old age home ≤ ₹25k/month
Import of services for charitable activities
Doctor/paramedic services by charitable trust
Name plate display (non-promotional)
Taxable
Renting commercial property
Advertising rights on trust premises
Admission tickets to events/shows
Serving food/drinks against consideration
Aerobics / dance / music fitness camps
Services provided TO trust
Name plate (if aimed at promoting donor's business)
Old age home > ₹25k/month
Quick test: Does the activity fall in PAEP? Yes → exempt. No → taxable, even if done by the trust. The trust's registration doesn't make everything it does exempt.
Religious Services — precincts of religious place
Memory hook: "Cheap rooms and small mandaps are for devotees. Expensive ones are for business events." Three thresholds drive all questions.
Exempt
Rooms below ₹1,000/day
Halls/Kalyanmandapam below ₹10,000/day
Shops for business below ₹10,000/month
Conduct of religious ceremony by any person
Religious pilgrimage by specified organisations (Kumaon Mandal Vikas, Haj Committee etc.)
Taxable
Rooms ≥ ₹1,000/day
Halls/Kalyanmandapam ≥ ₹10,000/day
Shops for business ≥ ₹10,000/month
Pattern: It's purely threshold-based. Just remember 1k/10k/10k (daily room / daily hall / monthly shop). At or above → taxable. Below → exempt. Community halls at ₹25,000/day are clearly ≥10k → taxable.
Agricultural Services
Memory hook: "Farm-to-market is exempt. Farm-to-factory is taxable." Anything that keeps the produce in its natural state = exempt. Anything that transforms it into a new product = taxable.
Exempt (natural state preserved)
Loading, unloading, packing, warehousing of agricultural produce / rice
Fumigation in warehouse
Supply of farm labour
Seed, soil, animal feed testing
Renting agro machinery or vacant land
Artificial insemination (non-horse livestock)
Pre-cooling, ripening, waxing fruits/vegetables
Pisciculture, Sericulture, Floriculture, Horticulture
Agricultural extension services
Warehousing of minor forest produce, spices, pulses (whole)
Taxable (transformation happens)
Milling paddy → rice
Processing sugarcane → jaggery
Potato → potato chips
Tomato → ketchup
Grinding, sterilizing, extraction for retail market
Warehousing of black tea / green tea / coffee
Warehousing of unmanufactured tobacco
Loading/unloading/warehousing of black/green tea, coffee
Services for de-husked or split pulses
Renting shops by APMC (non-agricultural)
Renting vacant land to stud farm (horses excluded)
Watch out: Spices, minor forest produce, whole pulses = exempt for warehousing. But unmanufactured tobacco = taxable (despite being a raw material). Tea and coffee = taxable for loading/warehousing but tea/coffee plants cultivation = exempt. Also, horses are always excluded from livestock exemptions.
Educational Services
Memory hook: Think in 3 levels. Level 1 (pre-school to Higher Secondary) = most exemptions apply. Level 2 (Degree/law-recognised) = partial exemptions. Level 3 (coaching/foreign/personality) = almost always taxable.
Exempt
BY educational institution: to students, faculty, staff
BY educational institution: entrance examination fee
TO pre-school/up to higher secondary: transport, catering (mid-day meals), security, cleaning
TO any educational institution: admission/exam conduct services
Online journals to degree/law-recognised institution (not pre-school)
CBSE / State Boards / NTA: conduct of exams
Boarding school (education + residence + food together)
IIM: long-duration (1 year+) degree/diploma programs
ITIs in designated trades (Apprenticeship Act)
Modular Employable Skill courses (NCVT approved)
Eligibility/migration certificate fees by institutions
Taxable
Transport to degree college (not pre-school/higher secondary)
Catering to degree/medical college students
Housekeeping to degree college
IIM: short-duration (under 1 year) executive programs
Private tuitions
Campus placement fees from employers
Coaching for board + competitive exams combined
Personality development / communication skills institutes
Renting property to coaching centre or higher secondary school
Foreign courses by private institutes
Third-party catering in college hostel mess
Development of course content (to any institution)
Key distinction: Services TO an institution providing only pre-school/up to higher secondary get transport + catering + security + cleaning exempt. For degree colleges: only admission/exam services and online journals (law-recognised only) are exempt. Everything else is taxable.
Healthcare Services
Memory hook: "Cure and care = exempt. Cosmetics and commerce = taxable." If it's in a recognised system of medicine and for a genuine medical need, it's exempt.
Exempt
Clinical establishments / authorised doctors / paramedics
Ambulance services
Mortuary services
Cord blood bank: preservation of stem cells
Food to in-patients as advised by doctors
Naturopathy / Ayurveda / Yoga / Homeopathy treatments
Plastic surgery for accident/congenital defect restoration
ART / IVF services
Palliative care at patient's home
Rooms for in-patients (below ₹5,000/day for non-ICU)
Veterinary clinic services for animals/birds
Bio-medical waste treatment
Rehabilitation by recognised professionals (RCI Act)
Taxable
Hair transplant
Cosmetic / plastic surgery (not for restoration)
Pranic healing (not a recognised system)
Food to non-admitted patients, attendants, visitors
Room ≥ ₹5,000/day (non-ICU)
Renting shops / auditoriums in hospital premises
Advertisement display in hospital
Trap question: Cord blood bank — despite being in a hospital, preservation of stem cells is taxable. It's a storage service, not a healthcare treatment. Similarly, pranic healing sounds medical but isn't recognised — taxable.
Government Services — BY and TO Government
Memory hook: "Sovereign acts = exempt. Business acts = taxable." Three always-taxable government services: (1) aircraft/vessel in ports, (2) transport of goods/passengers, (3) services to business entities other than sovereign functions.
Exempt (sovereign / welfare)
Panchayat / Municipality functions (Article 243G / 243W)
Basic postal services (postcard, book post, <10g envelopes)
Money orders, savings accounts, postal orders, pension
Passport, visa, driving license, birth/death certificate
Registration required under any law
Fines and penalties for statutory violations
General insurance where premium paid by State Govt
Old age homes (Govt run) ≤ ₹25k/month
Assignment of natural resource rights to individual farmer
Pure services to Govt for Panchayat/Municipality functions
Services where consideration ≤ ₹5,000
Taxable (commercial acts)
Speed post, express parcel to non-Govt
Distribution of mutual funds, bonds by post office
Passport application collection (commission basis)
Police / security to private business (RCM on recipient)
Services to business entities other than sovereign functions
Renting immovable property to business entities
Services in/around airport or port (aircraft/vessel)
Natural resources to companies (exploration royalty)
Works contract services to Govt
Composite supply with goods > 25% of value
The 3 always-taxables: Regardless of who the recipient is, these three government services are always taxable — (1) aircraft/vessel in airport/port, (2) transport of goods or passengers, (3) services like speed post or insurance to non-Govt. Memorise these three and you'll handle any trick question.
Transport Services — Passenger & Goods
Memory hook for passengers: AC = taxable, non-AC = exempt (with exceptions). E-commerce changes the game. Memory hook for goods: GTA with consignment note = potentially taxable. Without note = always exempt.
Exempt
Non-AC stage carriage (not via e-commerce)
Non-AC contract carriage (not via e-commerce, not tourism)
Railways: non-first class, non-AC (not via e-commerce)
Metro, monorail, tramway
Inland waterways
Economy class — NE states & Bagdogra
Metered cab / auto (not via e-commerce)
GTA: agricultural produce, milk, rice, flour, pulses, salt
GTA: relief material, military equipment, newspapers
GTA: single consignment ≤ ₹1,500 or single consignee ≤ ₹750
Transport by road (not GTA — no consignment note)
Air freight: outside India → customs clearance in India
Satellite launch (ISRO, Antrix, NSIL)
Taxable
AC stage carriage
Radio taxi
Tourism, charter, hire
Railways: first class or AC (not via e-commerce)
Cruise / leisure / charter vessels
Ropeway, cable car, aerial tramway
Non-AC carriage / auto via e-commerce operator
Air freight: India → outside India (after Sep 2022)
GTA: courier, angadia, express cargo
GTA: household effects, tea, sugar, petroleum, alcohol
GTA to unregistered person: Exempt (watch this!)
E-commerce trap: Any transport service notified under Sec 9(5) and provided via e-commerce operator becomes taxable, even if the same service by the operator directly is exempt. Non-AC auto by driver = exempt; via Ola/Uber = taxable. This is a very common MCQ trap.
Banking, Insurance & Financial Services
Memory hook: "Interest and discount = exempt (money for money). Fees and commissions = taxable (service charge on top)."
Exempt
Interest on loans, advances, deposits, overdrafts
Interest on corporate/fixed/savings deposits
Invoice / cheque discounting (to extent of discount)
Delayed payment charges (brokerage/margin)
Sale/purchase of foreign currency between banks
All services BY RBI
BSBD accounts under Jan Dhan Yojana
Acquiring bank: transactions ≤ ₹2,000 (credit/debit card)
Life insurance: NPS, Army/Navy/Air Force group schemes
Life insurance: Janashree Bima, Pradhan Mantri schemes
General insurance: govt schemes (PMFBY, RWCIS, etc.)
Atal Pension Yojana, State pension contribution collection
ESIC, EPFO, NPS Trust, SEBI, IRDAI services
Business correspondent to bank (rural area)
Taxable
Processing fees / service charges on loans
Documentation fees, broking charges
Bank guarantee commission
Minimum balance charges
Credit card interest (specifically excluded from exemption)
All services TO RBI
Service charges on derivative/forward contracts
One exception to remember: Interest is always exempt — EXCEPT credit card interest. That one is taxable. Every exam has a question trapping students on this. Also: RBI services = exempt. Services TO RBI = taxable. Flip it and you fail.

Brand ambassador trap: A classical dancer performing for ₹1.25 lakh = exempt. The same dancer performing as brand ambassador for a soap brand = taxable (regardless of amount or art form). The brand ambassador tag overrides everything. Also: RWA — if charges exceed ₹7,500, the ENTIRE amount is taxable, not just the excess.
